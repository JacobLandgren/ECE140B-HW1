Project Pivots

  1. From Inventory Tracker to Restocker
    The original concept for the product was a kitchen inventory tracker, which will know the inventory through the user scanning each item before storing it and before throwing it away. This was going to be achieved through either barcodes or RFID tags, but it was scrapped early on in the process due to complexity, barrier of entry, and the wasteful throwing away of RFID tags if they were used.

    The Zoom-in Pivot

    The Zoom-in Pivot applies to this pivot as the automated restocking was a possible feature of our initial inventory tracker, where when the product is thrown away, the user can opt in to it being automatically reordered.
  2. From RFID Tags to Barcodes
    After we narrowed down the product to just being used to automate the reordering of groceries, we had the option of either using a barcode, which the user would have to scan before throwing away the item, or an RFID tag that the user would have to attach to the item when they first buy and store it, which will then be sensed by a reader attached to the trashcan when it is thrown away. The RFID tag allowed the user to not have to worry about scanning the item manually when throwing it away, however, they had to attach and configure the tag initially, and then tag would get thrown away with the item, which is wasteful. Because of this, we opted to just use the item's barcode and have the user scan it before throwing it away.

    The Technology Pivot

    The technology pivot can be applied to this pivot, as we decided to go away from RFID to a barcode scanner. Although RFID is a more modern and cutting edge technology, it is not as applicable to this product and although it would be cool to play around with RFID tags, it is not the appropriate choice for this product.
  3. From Paid Hardware Component to Free
    For the initial revenue model for our product, we figured we would charge the user a one-time fee for the harware componenets (RPi, Barcode Scanner, etc.) as this would on paper generate more revenue for us. However, a TA (unsure of which one, sorry) suggested to have the hardware be free, as it would decrease the barrier of entry for our product, which we decided is the better option as the product is already such a seemingly luxiorious conveniency and people may be discouraged from adopting our product if there is some upfront cost. We decided to alter our revenue model to be more heavy on the per-order fees and subscription models as opposed to a general one-time charge for the hardware.

    The Value Capture Pivot

    The value capture pivot can be applied here as we changed our monetization strategy and revenue model based on a "customer's" insight, the customer being a TA, however, they likely are analyzing the product much like a potential customer would.
